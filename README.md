## How to use this list

This selection consists of one-word domains across a very wide TLD mix, with many names built on exact word-plus-extension combinations rather than mainstream extensions. Examples such as abstract.tax, vocal.graphics, chips.doctor, and technology.security show a clear pattern: the word carries most of the brand or category meaning, while the extension sharpens context. For founders, the key question is whether the full domain reads cleanly, feels credible, and is easy to recall. For buyers evaluating resale potential, the main checks are extension adoption, trademark exposure in examples like Audi.estate or McMuffin.cool, and whether the pairing is commercially usable rather than merely novel.
- Prioritize domains where word and TLD reinforce each other
- Check trademark exposure on branded terms before buying
- Niche TLDs can help clarity but may narrow buyer demand
- Memorability matters more here than broad extension familiarity

# Which data do we provide?

- **Status**: The current availability of the domain: Available, Expired, Expiring, For Sale, ...
- **Attract.**: The attractiveness score of the domain, evaluated by the word type, length, sentiment, popularity and brandability. The higher the score, the more attractive the domain is at eyes of people.
- **Demand**: The buyer pressure for domain names based on this word. 0 is no demand, and 100 is high probability of being sold soon. Formula: domains sold with this word / total domains tested with this word.
- **Length**: The word length of the domain (without the TLD)
- **Registrar**: The registrar of the domain.
- **Created**: The date the domain was registrered.
- **Expires**: The date the domain expires.

**Available on the Online Database only**:

- Estimated value (from GoDaddy)
- Business ideas (from GPT-5 highest version + web search)
- Domain Rating
- Backlinks
- Traffic volume
- Referring domains
- Organic keywords
